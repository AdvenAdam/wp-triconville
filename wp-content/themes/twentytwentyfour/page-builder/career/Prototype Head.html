Prototype Head

full time - Jepara / Kudus, Indonesia

overview

Responsible for printing all the required parts to put together prototypes of a product. In this role, a crucial part in the manufacturing process, ensuring all prototypes meet requirements and working with a supervisor to keep the assembly area safe.

responsibility

  • Develop and implement production plans to meet customer demand and optimize inventory levels.
  • Coordinate with sales, production, and procurement teams to ensure timely delivery of products.
  • Monitor and analyze production data to identify areas for improvement and cost reduction.
  • Manage and maintain accurate production schedules and capacity planning.
  • Collaborate with cross-functional teams to resolve production issues and ensure on-time delivery.
  • Lead and mentor a team of production planners and schedulers.
  • Implement and maintain production planning systems and tools.
  • Conduct regular meetings with key stakeholders to review production performance and address any issues.
  • Develop and implement strategies to improve production efficiency and reduce lead times.
  • Ensure compliance with company policies and procedures related to production planning and inventory management.


Qualification

  • 3 to 5 years experience in a similar job post.
  • Have a combination of technical, creative, and managerial skills.
  • Have a strong background in product development, engineering, or design, and be familiar with the tools and methods for creating and testing prototypes, such as sketching, wireframing, prototyping software, or 3D printing.
  • Have a good understanding of the user needs, preferences, and behavior, and be able to conduct and interpret user research and testing.
  • Have excellent communication, collaboration, and presentation skills, and be able to lead and motivate a team of diverse professionals.
  • Have good project management, problem-solving, and decision-making skills, and be able to handle multiple tasks and deadlines in a fast-paced environment.
  • Willing to be placed in a branch of our factory.
